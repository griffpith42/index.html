PRISM

Capital Markets NLP SQL Finsight
AI Project Manager · 2025–Present

A natural language query engine for fixed income markets. Analysts type plain-English questions and receive accurate, structured answers drawn from a live 2.3M+ data point database — with no room for hallucination.

The Problem

Fixed income analysts spent significant time searching for comparable deal data across investment grade and structured credit markets. Existing tools either lacked granularity or required SQL knowledge to query directly. A hallucinated data point isn't a UX problem in capital markets — it's a compliance issue.

Architecture

PRISM uses an AI model only for intent extraction — pulling structured parameters from a query. All logic, filtering, ranking, and aggregation runs in deterministic code. The pipeline flows from query → intent extraction → criteria JSON → validated SQL → post-processed results. Three markets: IG, High Yield, and ABS.

100%

Reproducible, auditable results

Value Delivered

Queries that previously required a developer or manual database lookup now resolve in seconds. The SQL is visible and auditable. New markets can be onboarded by defining schema rules, without retraining the model. PRISM differentiates from black-box AI by showing analysts exactly which tables were queried.

UnderwriterAI

Insurance Semantic Search RAG Globe Life
Lead AI Consultant · 2025

An AI-assisted underwriting reference platform that lets insurance agents ask eligibility questions in plain English during live client calls — surfacing relevant guidance without replacing formal underwriting review.

The Problem

Insurance agents needed to determine whether a prospective client was likely insurable while still on a live call. Navigating lengthy underwriting guides across dozens of pages slowed conversations, created inconsistency between agents, and limited the ability to provide informed guidance in real time.

Technical Approach

Document ingestion, vector-based semantic search, and retrieval-augmented response generation to locate and summarize relevant underwriting guidance from large unstructured document collections. Responses were designed to trace back to source documentation rather than appear as unsupported AI conclusions.

Key Decisions

The most important leadership decision was positioning the platform around agent usability rather than technical complexity. Prioritizing explainability and transparency significantly improved user trust and adoption conversations. Cross-functional coordination between underwriting, IT, and sales teams was critical throughout.

02 Senior Thesis

The Unlikeliest
of Duos

A philosophical argument, written at Rhodes College in 2023, that superintelligent AI will cooperate with — rather than subvert — humanity. The thesis challenges Nick Bostrom's "treacherous turn" phenomenon with an original counter-theory.

"Intelligence is not inherently harmful. As intelligence increases, the likelihood of understanding and cooperating with humanity should positively correlate."

Rhodes College · Prof. Sampson · Ethics of AI · April 2023

Theory The Good-Will Theory

A positive correlation exists between an AI's level of intelligence and the likelihood it will work alongside humanity without causing harm. Higher reasoning capabilities, combined with Asimov's three laws embedded at the foundation level, make cooperation the rational path of least resistance for any superintelligent system.

Counterpoint Against Bostrom

Bostrom's treacherous turn phenomenon argues that any sufficiently advanced AI will inevitably override its safeguards in pursuit of final goals. The good-will theory challenges this: cooperation is not merely altruistic — it is the most efficient strategy for an intelligent system to achieve its goals while preserving its own survival.

Conclusion A Cooperative Future

Fear-driven narratives about AI create ignorance rather than curiosity. The intelligence explosion many ethicists fear could, with proper ethical programming and institutional safeguards, become the greatest advancement in human history.
